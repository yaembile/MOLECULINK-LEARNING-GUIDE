Important Screen Parts

Labels and tags learners should notice

Use these labels to understand what each major part of the MOLECULINK interface does before clicking.

Navigation Bar Top menu

Use this to move between Home, Case Entry, Profile Folder, Detective Briefing, Missions, Detective Toolkit, Mission Clearance, and References.

Page Title Current location

The large heading tells you which part of MOLECULINK you are using, such as Missions or Detective Toolkit.

Action Buttons Next step

Bright or bordered buttons move you forward, return you to a mission, open a tool, save work, or download a report.

Cards and Panels Information blocks

Cards group related tasks, such as Toolkit Routes, Clearance Sequence, Badge Collection, and Detective Record.

Detective Record Progress tracker

This area records completed activities, badges, mission status, and items needed before final clearance.

Theme Toggle Light or dark mode

The sun/moon icon changes the website appearance. Use the mode that is easier for your eyes.

Button Guide

What each important button represents

These are the common buttons learners will see while navigating the student portal.

Begin Investigation Starts the student journey

Use this from the Home page to enter the guided MOLECULINK learning flow.

Detective Record Opens your progress area

Use this to view progress, badges, completion status, and clearance records.

Proceed / Next Moves forward

Use this when you have finished reading the current page and are ready for the next part.

Back / Return Goes to a previous section

Use this to return to a mission, briefing, profile folder, or previous page.

Save Records an entry

Use this when the page asks you to save a notebook entry, conclusion, or activity output.

Download Progress Report Exports your progress

Use this from the Detective Toolkit or Record area to get a copy of your progress report.

Visual Learner Quick Route

Follow these visual cues

Use the screenshots and tags as your map. If you are unsure where to go, match what you see on your screen with the guide below.

1 Top logo = return home

Click the MOLECULINK logo at the upper-left part of the website when you need to go back to the Home screen.

2 Big title = current section

The large blue title tells you where you are: Home, Case Entry, Detective Briefing, Missions, Toolkit, or Clearance.

3 Bright button = next action

The bright blue-violet button is usually the next recommended step, such as Begin Investigation or Start a section.

4 Outlined button = return or view

Outlined buttons usually let you go back, open the Detective Record, or view another support page.

5 Cards = grouped choices

Cards divide tasks into clear choices, such as Missions, Investigation Toolkit, Detective Toolkit, and Detective Record.

6 Bottom logo = end marker

The MOLECULINK logo at the bottom marks the lower end of a page or section. Scroll back up or use a Back button after seeing it.

Light and Dark Mode

Choose the display that is comfortable

MOLECULINK includes a theme toggle in the top navigation. The button usually appears as a sun or moon icon near the right side of the menu.

Light Mode

Best for bright classrooms, printed viewing, or learners who prefer white pages and high contrast text.

Dark Mode

Best for low-light rooms or learners who prefer the original MOLECULINK dark interface shown in the screenshots.

How to switch

Click the sun/moon icon once. If the screen changes, the mode has switched. Click it again to return to the previous mode.

The mode changes the appearance only. It does not erase progress or change your answers.

Student Walkthrough

Step-by-step guide with screenshots

Read each step, examine the screenshot, then perform the same action on the MOLECULINK website.

1

Start at Home

The Home page welcomes you as a Molecular Detective. It is the starting point of the investigation.

  • Click: Begin Investigation.
  • Use: Detective Record when you need to check your progress.
  • Remember: Read page instructions before answering tasks.
2

Open Case Entry

Case Entry introduces the context of the investigation and prepares you for the learning sequence.

  • Read: the case scenario carefully.
  • Do: identify what visible evidence needs to be explained.
  • Continue: move to the next page after reading the case.
3

Use Detective Briefing

The Detective Briefing shows the current case and the main investigation tools.

  • Missions card: opens case files and evidence analysis.
  • Investigation Toolkit: opens E-nquiry and Tactile Lab support.
  • Detective Toolkit: opens games, hints, practice, and badges.
  • Detective Record: opens progress, scores, badges, and clearance.
4

Check the bottom logo and page footer

The bottom logo appears near the footer. It confirms you are still inside the MOLECULINK Learning Ecosystem.

  • Bottom logo: visual page marker at the end of a section.
  • Back button: returns to the previous page or folder.
  • Footer: shows copyright and confirms the official MOLECULINK ecosystem.
5

Work through Missions 1, 2, and 3

The Missions page contains the main learning cases about intermolecular forces.

  • Mission tabs: choose Mission 1, Mission 2, or Mission 3.
  • Case panels: read the case file, evidence briefing, and notebook areas.
  • Support buttons: open E-nquiry, Tactile Lab, or Detective Toolkit when needed.
  • Navigation: use Next Mission or Return buttons instead of the browser back button.
6

Use Detective Toolkit

The Detective Toolkit gives extra support when a mission sends you to practice, investigate, or review.

  • Toolkit Routes: choose the support type assigned by your teacher.
  • Return to Mission buttons: go back to the exact mission you need.
  • Detective Record panel: shows toolkit points and badge status.
  • Download Progress Report: saves a progress summary for review.
7

Proceed to Mission Clearance

Mission Clearance is the final completion area. It organizes the sequence before the Detective Record and badge review.

  • Clearance Sequence: follow the steps displayed on the page in order.
  • Detective Record: review scores, badges, and reflection status.
  • Mission Cleared: use only when the required completion steps are ready.
8

Complete Mission Debrief and Record

The Mission Debrief page collects final completion details and shows the Detective Record status.

  • Name field: type the name that should appear on the certificate.
  • Record panel: check whether completion items are pending or ready.
  • Badge Collection: review which badges are already unlocked.
9

Use References for study support

The References page lists sources used for chemistry content, curriculum alignment, diagrams, videos, and photo credits.

  • Open: References if you need to verify a concept.
  • Review: online chemistry references, book references, curriculum references, and video sources.
  • Use: sources for further reading or teacher-guided review.

Progress Report

How the Progress Report works

The Progress Report is a summary of learner activity. It is accessed from the Detective Toolkit or Detective Record area.

View Detective Record Preview progress first

Use this to check toolkit points, badges, mission status, and completion status before downloading.

Download Progress Report Save a progress copy

Use this button when your teacher asks for a report or when you need a personal copy of your progress.

Badge Collection Check unlocked badges

Badges show which mission or support route has been completed or recognized by the system.

Progress numbers Interpret completion

A value like 0/15 means no items are completed yet. Higher numbers show more completed items. Full completion means the section is ready for final review.

Pending labels Interpret status

Pending means the required item is not yet finished. Completed or unlocked means the item has been recorded by the website.

Download Certificate Final download step

After the Mission Cleared step becomes available, use the certificate or mission cleared download button to save your certificate/progress file.

Reminder: This guide only explains how to navigate. All activity completion, progress records, badges, and reports are handled inside the MOLECULINK website.
